First Home Guarantee (FHBG)
Buy with just a 5% deposit — no lenders mortgage insurance
⌄

35,000

What it is: The federal government guarantees up to 15% of your loan, so you only need a 5% deposit and avoid paying lenders mortgage insurance (LMI). LMI can cost tens of thousands of dollars, so this is a significant saving.

Who is eligible:

  • Australian citizens or permanent residents aged 18+
  • First home buyers (or previous owner-occupiers who haven't owned a home in 10 years)
  • Annual taxable income ≤ $125,000 (singles) or ≤ $200,000 (couples)
  • Must be owner-occupier (not investment)
  • Property price caps apply by location (e.g. Sydney: $900,000; regional NSW: $750,000)

How to apply: Apply through one of the 32+ approved participating lenders (major banks and smaller lenders). You do not apply directly to the government.

What it is: A shared equity scheme where Housing Australia co-buys a portion of your home alongside you. You take a smaller mortgage, which means smaller repayments. You can buy the government's share over time.

Who is eligible:

  • Australian citizens aged 18+
  • First home buyers only
  • Annual taxable income ≤ $90,000 (singles) or ≤ $120,000 (couples)
  • Must be owner-occupier
  • Cannot own other property

Note: As of 2025, this scheme has passed the Senate and is expected to open to applicants through state and territory housing authorities. Check the official link for the latest rollout details.

Federal — superannuation
First Home Super Saver Scheme (FHSSS)
Save your deposit inside super and pay less tax
⌄
Max. per year
$15,000
Total withdrawal cap
$50,000
Tax on contributions
15%
Typical tax saving
$2,000–$6,000+

What it is: You make voluntary concessional (pre-tax) or non-concessional (after-tax) super contributions. When you're ready to buy, you apply to the ATO to release up to $50,000 (plus associated earnings). Because contributions are taxed at 15% instead of your marginal rate, you save on tax while building your deposit.

Who is eligible:

  • Australian residents aged 18+
  • Must never have previously requested a FHSSS release
  • Must intend to live in the property for at least 6 of the first 12 months you own it
  • Available to each individual in a couple separately (i.e. combined up to $100,000)

How it works: Make extra contributions to your super fund, then apply through myGov for a FHSSS determination from the ATO before signing a contract.
