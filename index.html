NSW first home buyer grants & schemes

If you're buying your first home in NSW, you may be eligible for the following government grants and schemes.

$10,000 Tax-free
First Home Owner (New Homes) Grant
A $10,000 grant for eligible first home buyers purchasing a newly built or substantially renovated home in NSW.
⌄
Official source — Revenue NSW ↗
CriteriaDetails
Grant amount$10,000 (tax-free, not means tested)
Property typeNewly built home, purchased off-the-plan, or substantially renovated — houses, townhouses, apartments, units
Price cap (purchase)Must not exceed $600,000
Price cap (land + build)Land value + building contract + variations must not exceed $750,000
Who can applyIndividual(s) aged 18+, Australian citizen or permanent resident, buying in personal names (not company or trust)
Previous ownershipYou and your spouse must not have previously owned residential property in Australia on or after 1 July 2000 and lived in it for 6+ continuous months
Residence requirementMust move in within 12 months of construction or purchase completion and live there for a minimum of 12 continuous months (for contracts signed after 1 July 2023)
Investment property exceptionIf you owned a residential property solely for investment purposes since 1 July 2000 you may still be eligible
Defence Force exemptionMembers of the permanent Australian Defence Force may be exempt from residence requirements if on the NSW electoral roll
Previous grantYou must not have previously received a First Home Owner Grant in any Australian state or territory
How it's paidPaid at settlement (for purchases) or at first progress payment/drawdown (for builds) — applied through your bank or financial institution
Can it be combined?Yes — can be used alongside the First Home Buyers Assistance Scheme and the First Home Guarantee
⚠ The grant is only available for new or substantially renovated homes. It is not available for established homes.

Up to $50,000 Federal scheme — available to all Australians
First Home Super Saver Scheme (FHSSS)
Boost your home deposit by saving through your super fund and benefit from lower tax rates.
⌄
Official source — ATO ↗
Key pointDetails
What it isA federal government scheme that lets you make voluntary super contributions to save for your first home deposit, then withdraw them at a lower tax rate
Maximum withdrawalUp to $15,000 per financial year, and up to $50,000 in total across all years
Eligible contributionsVoluntary contributions only — salary sacrifice (before-tax) or personal after-tax contributions. Employer's compulsory super (SG contributions) are not eligible
Tax benefitConcessional (before-tax) contributions are taxed at 15% in super, which is lower than most income tax rates. When withdrawn, tax is withheld at your marginal rate minus a 30% tax offset
Who can applyAustralian resident, aged 18 or over, never previously owned real property in Australia (exceptions apply for financial hardship)
Never owned propertyYou must not have previously owned residential property in Australia — this includes investment properties
Residence requirementYou must intend to live in the purchased property for at least 6 of the first 12 months of ownership
Property typeMust be a residential property — not a houseboat or mobile home. Vacant land is allowed if you intend to build and live there
How to accessApply for an FHSS determination through myGov linked to the ATO before signing a purchase contract. Then request a release. You must sign a contract within 12 months of the release request
If you don't buy in timeYou have 12 months after requesting release to sign a contract. If you don't, you can recontribute the funds to super (less tax withheld) or keep the money and pay a flat 20% FHSS tax
Can only use onceYou can only access the scheme once — even if you withdraw less than the maximum
Processing timeAllow up to 25 business days to receive funds from the ATO after a valid release request
⚠ You must receive your FHSS determination from the ATO before signing a contract to purchase your home. Signing a contract first will make you ineligible.
ℹ This is a federal scheme and applies to all eligible Australians regardless of state. It can be combined with NSW-specific grants and stamp duty exemptions.

Common questions

Answers to frequently asked questions about buying your first home in Australia.

In what way does maintaining a balance in an offset account shorten the effective life of a mortgage?
⌄
The Trick Imagine a $500,000 loan and $50,000 in your savings. The bank only charges interest on $450,000.
The Result Your monthly payment stays the same, but less goes to interest and more goes to paying off the actual debt.
The Goal Because you are eating away at the debt faster every month, the 30-year clock shrinks to 25 or 20 years.
The Analogy It's like making "extra payments" using the interest you saved — while keeping your cash available to spend if you need it.

Same repayment. By keeping $50,000 in an offset account, 53% more of every repayment reduces your loan balance — an extra $256 off your principal each month, at zero extra cost.
An offset account is a savings or transaction account linked to your home loan. Your bank calculates interest on the difference between your loan balance and whatever is sitting in your offset account. The more you keep in it, the less interest you accrue each month. Since your repayment amount stays fixed but more of it chips away at principal, the loan pays off years ahead of schedule — without locking away your money.
What is the process (step by step) of buying a home?
⌄
1The Financial Foundation
  • Determine "Comfortable" Repayments — Use the HomePocket calculator to define a monthly payment that fits your actual lifestyle, rather than just the maximum the bank will allow.
  • Check Incentives & Maximise Savings — Research federal schemes like the First Home Guarantee (FHBG) or Help to Buy, and consider saving your deposit through the First Home Super Saver Scheme (FHSSS) to benefit from lower tax rates.
  • Audit Upfront Costs — Calculate Stamp Duty and check for state-based exemptions or concessions available in your specific territory — see the Grants & schemes tab.
  • Secure Pre-approval — Obtain a formal "Approval in Principle" from a lender so you can bid with confidence and prove to agents you are a qualified buyer.
2The Search & Due Diligence
  • Inspect Properties — Attend open homes to evaluate the layout and location; look for immediate red flags like dampness, drainage issues, or visible cracks.
  • Build Your "A-Team" — Engage a solicitor or conveyancer early so they are ready to review contracts the moment you find a property you like.
  • Order Professional Reports — A Building Report and Strata Report (for apartments/townhouses) are highly recommended to ensure there are no hidden faults; a Pest Inspection is optional but worth considering for older properties.
3The Offer & Contract
  • Review the Contract of Sale — Have your solicitor perform a legal review to check for easements, historical caveats, or restrictive zoning.
  • Make the Offer
    Private Treaty: Negotiate the price and terms; where possible, include a "Subject to Finance" clause for protection.
    Auction: If you are the highest bidder and meet the reserve, the sale is Unconditional — you must pay the 10% deposit on the spot.
  • Exchange & Cooling-off — Once both parties sign, you have "Exchanged." Cooling-off periods vary by state (typically 3–5 days) and do not apply to properties bought at auction.
4Settlement
  • The Pre-Settlement Inspection — Visit the property 1–3 days before the final date to ensure it is in the agreed-upon condition and that all inclusions (like appliances) remain.
  • Settlement Day — Your bank and solicitor coordinate to transfer the remainder amount (whatever is needed after the deposit) to the seller's account.
  • Collect the Keys — Once the legal teams confirm the funds have landed, the agent will release the keys to your new home.
5The Long Game
  • Activate the Offset — Immediately move your remaining savings into your Offset Account to "shield" your loan from interest, effectively using your cash to pay off the debt years earlier.

Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI) $5,000 – $30,000+ Payable if your deposit is less than 20% of the property value and you are not using a government guarantee scheme (such as the First Home Guarantee). LMI protects the lender — not you — and is usually added to your loan amount. The exact cost depends on your loan-to-value ratio and loan size.
When can LMI be waived? ⌄
LMI can be waived in several situations:
1Government guarantee schemes

If you qualify for the First Home Guarantee (5% deposit) or another eligible government scheme, LMI is waived entirely — the government guarantees the remaining portion of the deposit with the lender. See the Grants & schemes tab for eligibility details.

2Family guarantee (guarantor loan)

A family member (typically a parent) can use equity in their own property to guarantee part of your loan, allowing you to borrow without LMI. At Westpac, this is called the Family Security Guarantee and is available for owner-occupied and investment purchases. Independent legal advice is required for all guarantors before proceeding.

3Eligible professions — lender LMI waivers

Some lenders waive LMI for borrowers in approved professions, even without a 20% deposit. Using Westpac as an example:

LVR allowed without LMI Eligible professions (Westpac example)
Up to 95% LVR (5% deposit) — no minimum income required Dentists, General Practitioners, Hospital-employed Doctors (Intern, Resident, Registrar, Staff Specialist), Medical Specialists
Up to 90% LVR (10% deposit) — minimum income $90,000/yr Audiologists, Chiropractors, Midwives, Occupational Therapists, Osteopaths, Physiotherapists, Podiatrists, Psychologists, Radiographers, Registered Nurses, Sonographers, Speech Pathologists, Optometrists, Pharmacists, Veterinary Practitioners
Up to 90% LVR (10% deposit) — income requirements apply Accountants, Legal professionals

Maximum loan amount for Westpac's medico 95% LMI waiver is $5 million ($7.5 million total lending). Eligibility is subject to Westpac's credit criteria and professional qualification verification.

⚠ LMI waiver policies vary by lender. Not all lenders offer profession-based waivers, and conditions change. Always confirm current eligibility directly with your lender or mortgage broker.
