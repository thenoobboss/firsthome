Deposit Growth Pathways

Three ways people build their first home deposit — pick the vibe that matches your personality and timeline.

Heads up: HomePocket doesn't give financial advice. This is just a look at how different people tackle the 'savings mountain.' Do your own research or talk to a pro before moving big money.
Low Risk 😴

The 'Sleep Easy' Path

The Vibe

You want your money exactly where you left it, plus a little extra. Peace of mind over max returns.

The 'What If?'

It's the safest way to save, but it's slow. You won't lose money in a crash, but house prices might climb faster than your interest rate can keep up.

Medium Risk 📈

The 'Slow & Steady' Path

The Vibe

You're okay with some bumps along the way if it means potentially beating what the bank offers you.

The 'What If?'

You need a 3–5 year runway. If the market dips right when you're ready to buy, you might have to wait for it to bounce back — or sell at a loss to hit your timeline.

High Risk 🎲

The 'Wildcard' Path

The Vibe

You're trying to speedrun your way to a deposit. High conviction, high stakes — not for the faint-hearted.

The 'What If?'

This is high-octane. You could double your money — or lose half of it in a weekend. Only for money you're genuinely prepared to lose.

First Home Super Saver Scheme (FHSSS)
Boost your home deposit by saving through your super fund and benefit from lower tax rates.
⌄
Official source — ATO ↗
Key pointDetails
What it isA federal government scheme that lets you make voluntary super contributions to save for your first home deposit, then withdraw them at a lower tax rate
Maximum withdrawalUp to $15,000 per financial year, and up to $50,000 in total across all years
Eligible contributionsVoluntary contributions only — salary sacrifice (before-tax) or personal after-tax contributions. Employer's compulsory super (SG contributions) are not eligible
Tax benefitConcessional (before-tax) contributions are taxed at 15% in super, which is lower than most income tax rates. When withdrawn, tax is withheld at your marginal rate minus a 30% tax offset
Who can applyAustralian resident, aged 18 or over, never previously owned real property in Australia (exceptions apply for financial hardship)
Never owned propertyYou must not have previously owned residential property in Australia — this includes investment properties

How to accessApply for an FHSS determination through myGov linked to the ATO before signing a purchase contract. Then request a release. You must sign a contract within 12 months of the release request
If you don't buy in timeYou have 12 months after requesting release to sign a contract. If you don't, you can recontribute the funds to super (less tax withheld) or keep the money and pay a flat 20% FHSS tax
Can only use onceYou can only access the scheme once — even if you withdraw less than the maximum

No LMI · No income cap · Unlimited places
Australian Government 5% Deposit Scheme
Buy a home with just a 5% deposit and avoid paying Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI). Formerly known as the Home Guarantee Scheme.
⌄
Usage warning — owner-occupier only
⚠ You must live in the property as your primary residence. To maintain the government guarantee and avoid being forced to pay LMI retrospectively, the home cannot be converted into a rental property until you have paid off at least 20% of the property value (reached 80% LVR). Converting to an investment property before this threshold triggers an LMI liability that the scheme was designed to avoid.
⚠ The government guarantee is not a cash payment — it is a promise to the bank. If you default on the loan, the government covers the lender's shortfall, but you remain fully liable to the government for that amount.
Official source — First Home Buyers Australia ↗
How it works
When using this scheme, you contribute 5% of the property's value as a deposit. The Australian Government provides a guarantee for up to 15% of the value. This means you borrow the remaining 95% of the purchase price from the bank. Because of the 15% guarantee, the bank treats your total security as 20% — which is why you avoid the LMI fee despite having a 95% Loan-to-Value Ratio (LVR).
Eligibility requirements
CriteriaDetails
Who can applyAustralian citizen or permanent resident, aged 18 or over
Income capNo income cap — available to all eligible buyers regardless of income
Property typeNew or existing residential homes — houses, townhouses, apartments, land + build contracts
Price cap (Sydney & major regional centres)Up to $1,500,000
Price cap (other NSW regions)Varies — check the official price cap table for your postcode
Places availableUnlimited — no waitlist, no annual cap
First home buyer requirementAt least one applicant must be a first home buyer (joint applications permitted where one buyer has previously owned)
Participating lendersMust apply through an approved participating lender — not all banks participate

What is the process of buying a home?

From saving your deposit to collecting the keys — here's every stage of the home-buying journey in order.

1
Get financially ready
  • Build your deposit (typically 10–20% of purchase price)
  • Check your credit score and clear high-interest debt
  • Save for stamp duty, legal fees, and inspections
  • Understand your borrowing capacity
2
Get pre-approved for a home loan
  • Compare lenders and loan types (variable, fixed, split)
  • Submit income proof, bank statements, and ID
  • Pre-approval is typically valid for 3 months
  • Use our Borrow tab to estimate your capacity
3
Search and inspect properties
  • Define your must-haves vs nice-to-haves
  • Attend open homes — bring a checklist
  • Research suburb median prices and rental yields
  • Get a building & pest inspection before committing
4
Make an offer or bid at auction
  • Private treaty: negotiate price with the agent
  • Auction: register to bid — unconditional on the fall of the hammer
  • Always have a conveyancer review the contract first
  • Cooling-off period: 5 business days in NSW (does not apply at auction)
5
Exchange, settle, and collect keys 🎉
  • Exchange contracts: pay the 10% deposit (or negotiated amount)
  • Settlement period: typically 42 days in NSW
  • Final inspection the day before settlement
  • Settlement day: funds transfer, title transfers — you're a homeowner!
Category A · High Priority

Incentives & Maximising Savings

What government grants and schemes can first home buyers access?
⌄
FHBAS (NSW) $0 stamp duty on properties up to $800,000. Concessional rate $800k–$999k. Can save up to ~$31,000.
FHOG (NSW) $10,000 grant for new builds or off-the-plan purchases up to $600,000. Must be your principal place of residence.
First Home Guarantee Federal scheme: buy with just 5% deposit, no LMI. 35,000 places/year. Income caps apply.
FHSS Scheme Save inside super taxed at 15% instead of your marginal rate. Up to $50,000 releasable at purchase.
Used strategically, these schemes can save first-home buyers tens of thousands of dollars. Eligibility depends on income, property price, and prior ownership history. See the full breakdown in the Grants & schemes tab.
How does the First Home Super Saver Scheme (FHSS) work?
⌄
1Make Voluntary Contributions
  • Contribute up to $15,000 per financial year into your super via salary sacrifice or after-tax contributions.
  • Total amount you can ever release is capped at $50,000 across all years (as of 1 July 2022).
2Benefit from Lower Tax
  • Salary sacrifice contributions are taxed at 15% instead of your marginal rate (which may be 32.5%+). This difference is your real saving.
  • Earnings inside super are also capped at 15%, compounding the benefit over time.
3Release Funds When Ready to Buy
  • Apply to the ATO to release your FHSS funds once you've found a property.
  • You must sign a purchase contract within 12 months of your FHSS determination date (extensions available).
⚠ FHSS is most effective for higher-income earners with a longer savings timeline. For lower incomes, a High-Interest Savings Account may be simpler and more flexible. Always consult a financial adviser.
Category B

Determine Comfortable Repayments

In what way does maintaining a balance in an offset account shorten the effective life of a mortgage?
⌄
The Trick Imagine a $500,000 loan and $50,000 in your savings. The bank only charges interest on $450,000.
The Result Your monthly payment stays the same, but less goes to interest and more goes to paying off the actual debt.
The Goal Because you are eating away at the debt faster every month, the 30-year clock shrinks to 25 or 20 years.
The Analogy It's like making "extra payments" using the interest you saved — while keeping your cash available to spend if you need it.

Same repayment. By keeping $50,000 in an offset account, 53% more of every repayment reduces your loan balance — an extra $256 off your principal each month, at zero extra cost.
An offset account is a savings or transaction account linked to your home loan. Your bank calculates interest on the difference between your loan balance and whatever is sitting in your offset account. The more you keep in it, the less interest you accrue each month. Since your repayment amount stays fixed but more of it chips away at principal, the loan pays off years ahead of schedule — without locking away your money.

Process & Legal

What is the step-by-step process of buying a home?
⌄
1The Financial Foundation
  • Determine Comfortable Repayments — Use the HomePocket calculator. Aim for what fits your lifestyle, not the bank's maximum.
  • Check Incentives & Grants — FHBG, FHBAS, FHSS — see the Grants tab for your state.
  • Audit Upfront Costs — Stamp duty, LMI, conveyancer, building report. Budget all of these on top of your deposit.
  • Secure Pre-approval — Get a formal "Approval in Principle" so you can bid with confidence.
2Search & Due Diligence
  • Inspect Properties — Look for red flags: dampness, drainage issues, visible cracks.
  • Build Your A-Team — Engage a solicitor or conveyancer before you need them.
  • Order Professional Reports — Building report (and strata report for apartments) before committing.
3Offer & Contract
  • Review the Contract — Solicitor checks easements, caveats, zoning.
  • Make the Offer — Private Treaty: negotiate with "Subject to Finance." Auction: unconditional, 10% deposit on the day.
  • Exchange & Cooling-off — 3–5 days in most states. Does not apply to auction purchases.
4Settlement
  • Pre-Settlement Inspection — Confirm the property is in agreed condition 1–3 days before.
  • Settlement Day — Bank and solicitor transfer the balance to the seller.
  • Collect the Keys 🎉 — Funds confirmed, agent releases keys.
5The Long Game
  • Activate the Offset — Move remaining savings into your Offset Account immediately to shield your loan from interest and pay it off years earlier.

When can LMI be waived?⌄
LMI can be waived in several situations:
1Government guarantee schemes

If you qualify for the First Home Guarantee (5% deposit), LMI is waived entirely — the government guarantees the remaining portion with the lender.

2Family guarantee (guarantor loan)

A family member uses equity in their own property to guarantee part of your loan, eliminating LMI. Independent legal advice required for all guarantors.

3Eligible professions

Some lenders waive LMI for approved professions. Using Westpac as an example:

LVR without LMIEligible professions (Westpac example)
Up to 95% LVR — no min. incomeDentists, GPs, Hospital Doctors, Medical Specialists
Up to 90% LVR — min. $90k/yrAudiologists, Nurses, Physiotherapists, Psychologists, Pharmacists, Optometrists, Vets and more
Up to 90% LVR — income req. applyAccountants, Legal professionals
⚠LMI waiver policies vary by lender. Always confirm eligibility directly with your lender or broker.

On a typical $750,000 NSW first home purchase, non-scheme buyers face approx.:
  • Transfer duty: ~$28,800 (or $0 if FHBAS eligible)
  • Solicitor: ~$1,500
  • Mortgage registration + title transfer: ~$325
